Problem Statement: Take two ferromagnetic rings of different radii and tie them by a string. Connect another string to the larger ring at the samepoint. Now holding the whole construction by the second string, you can suspend it in the air by a magnet placed above (see link below). Being held only by these vertical strings, the rings are free to rotate around the vertical axis. Investigate their rotational motion based on the parameters of the system (magnetic permeability of the rings, moments of inertia, positions, thickness, etc). What happens if you replace the central ring with a disk?

"Theory" lists our analysis of the system. Essentially we write down a Lagrangian of the coupled system with a potential that describes the magnetic interaction. To obtain this potential, we approximate the ferromagnetic rings to consist of tangentially oriented dipoles. The total magnetic interaction is thus the integral over the interactions between all pairs of dipoles.

"Method" lists our experimental techniques and results to analyze the system. A video link of our original experiments can be found here: https://youtu.be/qKzgtxwLCWc
